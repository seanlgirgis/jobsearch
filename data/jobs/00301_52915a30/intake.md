Role Summary

The Performance Architect is responsible for defining and driving the enterprise-wide performance engineering strategy across applications and platforms. This role combines deep technical expertise with strategic, consultative thinking to ensure scalable, resilient, and high-performing systems aligned with business objectives. The architect partners with technology and business stakeholders to embed performance engineering into the organization’s DNA, influencing design decisions, governance models, and long-term roadmaps.

 

Key Responsibilities

Define and own the enterprise performance engineering strategy, frameworks, and best practices.

Act as a trusted advisor to business and technology leaders on performance, scalability, and reliability considerations.

Provide architectural guidance to ensure performance is built into systems from design through production.

Lead performance assessments across applications, identifying systemic bottlenecks and optimization opportunities.

Establish and drive performance governance models, including SLA/SLI/SLO definitions aligned with business outcomes.

Standardize tools, methodologies, and processes across multiple applications and portfolios.

Drive continuous performance improvement initiatives across release cycles and transformation programs.

Design and oversee execution of advanced performance testing strategies (load, stress, endurance, scalability).

Build and present executive-level insights, dashboards, and performance reports to stakeholders.

Collaborate cross-functionally with engineering, operations, infrastructure, and business teams to integrate performance into the SDLC and DevOps pipelines.

Mentor and guide performance engineers, fostering a culture of engineering excellence and proactive optimization.
