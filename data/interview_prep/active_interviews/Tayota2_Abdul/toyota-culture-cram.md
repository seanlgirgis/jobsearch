# Toyota Culture Cram (12-Hour Version)

## 1) What are the two pillars of the Toyota Way?
The Toyota Way rests on Continuous Improvement (Kaizen) and Respect for People. Kaizen means no process is ever final; teams continuously improve quality and outcomes. Respect for People means trusting, developing, and listening to employees while serving customers, partners, and communities responsibly.

## 2) What is genchi genbutsu, and how do you apply it in engineering?
Genchi genbutsu means "go and see" the real work directly. In engineering, I apply it by checking logs, traces, alerts, pipeline runs, and user impact firsthand before deciding on fixes. It prevents decisions based only on assumptions or secondhand reports.

## 3) What does Toyota's long-term view mean for engineering decisions?
Toyota plans in decades, not quarters. In engineering terms, that means prioritizing reliability, safety, maintainability, and capability-building over short-term flashy wins.

## 4) What is Mobility for All in one sentence?
Mobility for All is Toyota's commitment to make freedom of movement accessible to everyone, including people limited by disability, age, geography, or economic constraints.

## 5) Why is Mobility for All more than marketing?
Toyota backs it with execution: adaptive mobility support programs, long-term foundation investments, and ongoing R&D initiatives that test real mobility solutions.

## 6) Give two concrete examples that prove execution.
- U.S. mobility assistance program: up to $1,000 reimbursement for qualifying adaptive equipment on new Toyota vehicles, with extended financing options through Toyota Financial Services.
- Toyota Mobility Foundation plus active R&D programs like iBot, Exo-Wheel prototypes, and Woven City.

## 7) Why Toyota Financial Services specifically?
Toyota Financial Services combines financial scale with Toyota's people-first mission. My 8 years at Citi prepared me for regulated, high-reliability financial systems, and I can apply that experience to improve platform reliability, risk control, and customer trust at TFS.

## 8) How do you demonstrate Respect for People as an engineering lead?
I run blameless incident reviews, focus on process gaps instead of blame, and make sure junior engineers are heard in design decisions. This builds trust and improves outcomes like faster recovery and fewer repeat issues.

## 9) How would you apply kaizen to a data team in 90 days?
- Days 1-30: baseline failure rate, MTTR, alert quality, ownership gaps.
- Days 31-60: high-impact fixes (retry/idempotency hardening, alert tuning, retrospectives).
- Days 61-90: standardize with templates, CI/CD guardrails, runbooks.
Track metrics: lower failures, faster MTTR, fewer repeat incidents.
